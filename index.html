Mathematics for Evolutionary Game Theory

A comprehensive course building from mathematical foundations to evolutionary game theory, replicator dynamics, social value orientations, and Ostrom's institutional analysis.

Start Learning →

Why This Course?

Evolutionary game theory (EGT) and replicator dynamics are powerful frameworks for understanding how strategies evolve in populations — from biological organisms to human institutions. But these frameworks require mathematical tools that aren't always taught together in one place.

This course is designed for researchers and students interested in social value orientations and Elinor Ostrom's institutional analysis who want to build the mathematical fluency needed to engage deeply with evolutionary game theory. We start from the very basics and build systematically toward the mathematics of cooperation, institutions, and social dynamics.

How the Math Connects

Here's how each mathematical topic feeds into evolutionary game theory and institutional analysis:

Mathematical Tool Used In EGT / Institutional Analysis For...
Functions & Algebra Expressing payoff functions, fitness functions, utility functions
Matrices Payoff matrices, transition matrices, strategy interaction tables
Eigenvalues Stability analysis of equilibria, classifying fixed points
Derivatives Rate of change of strategy frequencies, optimization conditions
Differential Equations The replicator equation itself, population dynamics models
Fixed Points & Stability Finding equilibrium strategy mixes, determining if they're stable (ESS)
Probability Mixed strategies, stochastic dynamics, finite populations
Expected Value Computing average fitness/payoff under mixed strategies
Non-Rivalry & Anti-Rivalry Digital commons dynamics, network effects, how value increases with use in knowledge commons
